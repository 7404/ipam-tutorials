0. Supervised, online learning: basics


In this section, we apply basic supervised learning, in online settings. For the purpose of the tutorial, we use 3 public datasets:


  • MNIST: the famous hand-written character recognition dataset
  • CIFAR10: the other quite famous object recognition dataset
  • SVHN: the less famous, quite recent dataset of house numbers


All three datasets have pretty much the same characteristics in terms of dimensionality:


  • inputs are 32x32(x3)
  • outputs (targets) are 10-dimensional


But differ in terms of statistics and complexity. In particular, the house numbers dataset is significantly harder than the MNIST dataset, due to the much larger variance in the backgrounds.
